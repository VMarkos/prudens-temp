Chess's Language

Prudens's Chess language provides three built-in predicates:

  • legalMove(Piece, Color, FromCol, FromRow, ToCol, ToRow, Flags), describing a legal move of a piece of type Piece of color Color, from the cell at column FromCol and at row FromRow to the cell at column ToCol and at row ToRow. Also, the move may be accompanied by one or more flags (as discussed below);
  • at(Col, Row, Piece, Color) represents a cell at column Col and row Row with a piece Piece of color Color on it;
  • move(Piece, Color, FromCol, FromRow, ToCol, ToRow, Flags) corresponds to a move action and should be used in order to indicate preference regarding making a certain move or not.

The values the various arguments might take are as follows:

  • Rows range from 1 to 8;
  • Columns range from a to h;
  • Pieces are denoted using the usual FEN notation, i.e., p for pawn, n for knight etc;
  • Color is denoted by b for black and w for white;
  • Move flags follow the chess.js library specifications, as found here.

A Simple Example

A very simple policy could be the following one: 

							@KnowledgeBase
                                Dummy :: legalMove(Piece, Color, FromCol, FromRow, ToCol, ToRow, Flags) implies move(Piece, Color, FromCol, FromRow, ToCol, ToRow, Flags);`

That is, any legal move should be considered. Evidently, the above is not a sustainable strategy, since it is actually equivalent to playing at random.
